# PackGuardian — Pilot Objection Handling

Sales conversations with pet care operators tend to produce the same 12 objections. Here's how to handle each one.

---

## Objection 1: "My staff won't use it — they're too busy."

**Why they say it:** Past experiences with software that took 10+ minutes to learn and multiple screens to navigate.

**Response:**
"I hear that. The mobile intake takes under 60 seconds — faster than filling out a paper incident form. There's a voice mode where they can just speak what happened. We built this specifically because kennel staff don't have time to use complicated software. If it's not faster than what you're doing now, we'll fix it."

**Proof point:** Voice entry → AI extraction → submit in 3 taps.

---

## Objection 2: "We already track incidents in spreadsheets / Google Forms."

**Why they say it:** They've built a system they're comfortable with, even if it's slow.

**Response:**
"How long does it take to compile your OSHA 300 log at year end? How do you know if an incident from 6 months ago had a corrective action? Can you tell me right now which of your locations has had the most repeat incidents this year?"

If they can't answer quickly: "That's what PackGuardian makes instant."

**Proof point:** Show the executive briefing page. Ask them to guess how long it would take to assemble that report manually.

---

## Objection 3: "We don't have OSHA issues — our incidents are minor."

**Why they say it:** They underestimate recordability requirements or haven't been inspected.

**Response:**
"Under 29 CFR 1904, you're required to record any work-related injury that requires medical treatment beyond first aid, results in restricted duty, or causes days away from work. A dog bite that needs stitches is recordable. A back strain from lifting a large dog is recordable. Many pet care operators don't realize their exposure until they're audited."

**Don't be preachy** — follow up with: "PackGuardian just makes the determination automatically, so you don't have to."

---

## Objection 4: "This is too expensive for what it is."

**Why they say it:** They're comparing to nothing, or they haven't had a costly incident yet.

**Response:**
"A single workers' compensation claim costs an average of $40,000–$60,000 to settle. Poor documentation increases that by 35–60%. One well-documented incident pays for two years of PackGuardian. We're not a cost — we're documentation insurance."

**At $49/location/month:** "We're under $2 a day per location. That's less than one cup of coffee per kennel."

---

## Objection 5: "We tried software before and stopped using it."

**Why they say it:** Real experience with system abandonment. Valid concern.

**Response:**
"That's a fair concern. Most incident software was built for manufacturing — it requires training, customization, and a dedicated EHS coordinator. PackGuardian is built for how pet care actually works: a phone in hand, a dog just bit someone, and no time for menus. Can I show you the mobile intake flow? It's 60 seconds."

**Don't skip the live demo.** This objection dies when they see the UX.

---

## Objection 6: "We're too small / only have 2 locations."

**Why they say it:** They think it's enterprise software.

**Response:**
"PackGuardian starts making sense at 2 locations — which is exactly when OSHA violations and repeat incidents start hiding from view. A 2-location operator with 15 staff has the same OSHA obligations as a 50-location chain. The paperwork doesn't scale down just because you do."

---

## Objection 7: "I need to check with my insurance carrier / attorney / HR first."

**Why they say it:** Decision paralysis, or they genuinely need approval.

**Response:**
"That's wise. When you talk to them, ask specifically about documentation practices for OSHA 300 compliance and whether your current process creates liability exposure if an incident isn't recorded. That's the conversation PackGuardian is designed to support."

**Don't push** — offer to send the FAQ and ROI estimator to share with their team.

---

## Objection 8: "What happens to our data if we stop using it?"

**Why they say it:** Legitimate data portability concern.

**Response:**
"You can export your incident data and OSHA records at any time. We don't hold your data hostage. Your historical records are yours, and we'll help you export them in a usable format if you ever decide to leave."

---

## Objection 9: "Our staff doesn't want to be tracked."

**Why they say it:** Fear of surveillance culture, or staff pushback concern.

**Response:**
"PackGuardian isn't surveillance — it's documentation. The same information that goes in a paper incident report goes into PackGuardian. The difference is that it doesn't get lost, it's findable in a week, and it protects your staff as much as it protects you. If a worker files a claim, complete documentation protects both parties."

**Language note:** Don't use "monitoring" or "tracking." Use "documentation" and "investigation support."

---

## Objection 10: "We already have a kennel management system / PetExec / Gingr / etc."

**Why they say it:** They think this is a CRM replacement.

**Response:**
"PackGuardian doesn't replace your kennel management software — it adds the safety layer that those tools don't have. Your KMS handles bookings, vaccination records, and billing. PackGuardian handles what happens when something goes wrong: incident documentation, investigation, OSHA compliance, and risk visibility. They work alongside each other."

---

## Objection 11: "Can we pilot with just one location?"

**Why they say it:** Cautious decision-making. Also: budget concern.

**Response:**
"Absolutely. Most pilots start with 1–3 locations and expand. We recommend your highest-incident-volume location as the pilot site so you see real value quickly. If it doesn't work for that location, it won't work anywhere — and you'll know within 30 days."

---

## Objection 12: "We need integration with [specific system]."

**Why they say it:** Real workflow dependency concern.

**Response:**
"We have an open API and webhook support. During pilot, most operators find that PackGuardian handles the safety documentation layer independently — incident data doesn't usually need to flow into booking systems or payroll. What specific integration are you thinking about?"

If it's something reasonable (Workers' Comp carrier reporting, HR system): "That's on our roadmap. Let's pilot first, and if adoption is strong, we'll prioritize it."

---

## The Three Closes

**For operators who've had a bad incident:**
"You know how painful that was to document. PackGuardian would have had it ready in the time you spent on email chains."

**For operators who haven't had a serious incident:**
"Your first OSHA recordable is coming. The question is whether you're ready when it does."

**For PE-backed / multi-location chains:**
"Your corporate buyers and insurers are going to ask for standardized incident documentation. PackGuardian is the answer to that question."
