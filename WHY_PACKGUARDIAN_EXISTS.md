# Why PackGuardian Exists

## The Problem

Pet care operations — kennels, daycares, boarding facilities, grooming salons, veterinary boarding — handle real physical risk every day.

Dogs bite. Staff get hurt. Animals escape. Chemical exposures happen. Equipment fails.

When these things happen, most pet care businesses respond the same way:
- Someone fills out a paper form (or a generic Google Form)
- Someone sends an email chain
- Someone tries to remember if they need to call OSHA
- The incident gets filed somewhere, or lost
- Nothing changes. The same incident happens again.

## The Regulatory Reality

OSHA 29 CFR Part 1904 requires employers to:
- Record work-related injuries and illnesses on Form 300
- Maintain individual incident reports (Form 301)
- Post annual summaries (Form 300A) February through April
- Retain records for 5 years

Most pet care businesses are doing this incorrectly or not at all.

Not because they don't care. Because they don't have the tools.

A kennel with 15 staff members and 4 locations doesn't have a safety department. They have a head kennel manager, a part-time office coordinator, and a lot of dogs. They're not going to figure out 1904.29 on their own.

## The Operational Reality

Beyond regulatory compliance, there's an operational problem.

Incidents repeat.

The same dog causes conflict again. The same drain backs up near the same walkway. The same grooming station overheats. The same shift is understaffed on Tuesdays.

These patterns are visible in the incident data — but nobody sees the data because it's in email chains, paper forms, and manager memories.

## What PackGuardian Is

PackGuardian is the operational safety layer for multi-location pet care.

It is not:
- Generic incident management software
- An OSHA "compliance tool" that exists to check boxes
- Another dashboard that nobody uses

It is:
- A fast mobile intake that field staff will actually use during a chaotic situation
- Automatic OSHA recordability determination — the system decides, not the paperwork
- Investigation infrastructure: corrective actions, witness statements, evidence, timeline
- Pattern detection that surfaces recurring risks before they escalate
- A risk scoring system that tells operators which locations need attention today
- An executive view that makes multi-location oversight possible without micromanagement

## Who It's For

**Primary:** Multi-location pet care operators — kennels, daycares, boarding, grooming chains with 3–50 locations

**Secondary:** Safety coordinators managing OSHA compliance for pet care organizations

**Tertiary:** Veterinary boarding operations and franchise groups needing consistent safety documentation across locations

## The Core Insight

OSHA compliance is the regulatory floor. Operational safety is the ceiling.

PackGuardian starts with compliance (because operators need to stay out of trouble) and builds toward operational excellence (because operators want to provide safe environments for their staff, their animals, and their clients).

The same incident report that files the OSHA 300 log also:
- Creates a case for investigation
- Triggers corrective actions
- Feeds the pattern detection engine
- Updates the location health score
- Notifies the safety coordinator

One report. One system. No paper.

## Why Now

The pet care industry is growing rapidly and becoming institutionalized.

Multi-location operators, private equity-backed chains, and franchise groups are replacing single-owner kennels. These operators need enterprise-grade safety infrastructure but can't afford enterprise software complexity.

OSHA enforcement of pet care operations has increased. Dog bite liability is rising. Workers' compensation claims in pet care are above industry average.

The moment to build this platform is now — before the market has a dominant solution and before the regulatory environment forces a crisis response.
