## Slide 2 — The Problem

Pet care is physical work. Things go wrong.

Dogs bite. Staff get hurt. Animals escape. Chemical exposures happen. Equipment fails.

When they do, most operations respond with:
→ Paper forms that get lost
→ Email chains that don't get followed up
→ Spreadsheets that are wrong
→ Memory that fades

**OSHA doesn't care about your process. They care about your records.**

And your workers' comp insurer doesn't care about your intentions. They care about your documentation.

## Slide 3 — The Scale of the Problem

**OSHA 300 recordability requirements** apply to every employer with 11+ employees.

**Dog bites requiring ER care:** OSHA recordable.
**Employee slip/fall with restricted duty:** OSHA recordable.
**Chemical exposure requiring hospitalization:** OSHA recordable.

The average pet care operator **doesn't know** if their incidents are being recorded correctly.

OSHA fine per unrecorded incident: up to $15,625.
Workers' comp cost increase from poor documentation: 35–60%.

## Slide 6 — Why Pet Care (The Insight)

Pet care is unlike any other industry that needs safety operations software.

- **No EHS department** — a 10-location kennel chain has a GM, not a safety officer
- **High physical risk** — dogs, chemicals, lifting, wet surfaces, sharp objects
- **Young, non-technical workforce** — staff aren't going to navigate complex software
- **OSHA obligations** that most operators don't fully understand
- **Growing and consolidating** — private equity is entering, franchises are scaling

**The market exists. The software doesn't.** Generic tools are built for manufacturing. EHS enterprise is built for Fortune 500. Nobody built for pet care.

Until now.
