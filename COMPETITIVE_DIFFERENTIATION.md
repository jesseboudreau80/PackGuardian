# PackGuardian — Competitive Differentiation

## The Competitive Landscape

PackGuardian doesn't have a direct competitor purpose-built for pet care safety operations. The alternatives operators consider fall into three buckets:

1. **Generic incident management software** (Incident IQ, Origami, Cority, Riskonnect)
2. **EHS enterprise platforms** (Intelex, EHS Insight, ProcessMAP, Velocity EHS)
3. **Paper / DIY** (Google Forms, spreadsheets, email)

## Our Key Differentiators

### 1. Pet Care DNA
Every incident type, follow-up question, OSHA determination, and operational metric was built for pet care environments. Competitors require operators to configure their industry — we already know it.

### 2. 60-Second Field Intake
The mobile intake is designed for kennel staff — voice-first, works offline, minimal taps. No other platform gets this right for field workers.

### 3. Automatic OSHA Determination
Treatment type + work restriction = OSHA recordability. The system decides. No OSHA manual required. No guesswork.

### 4. Center Health Scoring
Multi-location operators can see which locations need attention today without building reports. No other pet care tool has this.

### 5. Investigation Intelligence
Recurrence patterns, contributing factor analysis, witness synthesis, corrective action tracking — an investigation workflow, not just a form.

### 6. Time to Value
30 minutes from signup to first incident report. EHS enterprise tools take quarters.

## What We Deliberately Don't Do

We intentionally avoid:
- HR system functionality (payroll, scheduling, performance management)
- General business management (booking, billing, inventory)
- Veterinary medical records
- Customer-facing communication
- Training management (placeholder, not built)

This makes us better at what we do. Every feature serves one goal: operational safety documentation and visibility.

## How to Position in a Competitive Conversation

**If they're considering generic software:**
"That tool was built for manufacturing. When you tell it 'a dog bit someone' it treats that the same as 'a forklift accident.' PackGuardian knows the difference — and knows what OSHA requires for each."

**If they're considering enterprise EHS:**
"Does that tool work from a phone in 60 seconds with no WiFi? Will your kennel techs use it? Enterprise EHS is built for companies with safety departments. You have a head manager."

**If they're on spreadsheets:**
"How long does it take you to compile your OSHA 300 log? When was the last time you checked if it's actually complete? One OSHA audit could expose every gap your spreadsheet has been hiding."

## Win Conditions

PackGuardian wins when:
- The prospect has had a painful incident or audit
- The prospect manages 3+ locations
- The prospect can't tell you immediately which of their locations has the most open corrective actions
- The prospect is growing (opening new locations) and knows their current process won't scale

PackGuardian loses when:
- The prospect needs an HR system (we're not that)
- The prospect needs a kennel management system (we're not that either)
- The prospect is a single-location owner-operator with no staff
