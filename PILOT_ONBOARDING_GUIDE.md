# PackGuardian — Pilot Onboarding Guide
**Version:** Phase 19 — Pilot Launch  
**For:** Pilot operators and their team members

---

## Welcome

PackGuardian is your operational safety platform for pet care.

You can use it to:
- Report incidents from your phone in under a minute
- Track investigations from start to close
- Stay OSHA-compliant automatically
- Spot patterns across locations before they repeat

This guide gets you from zero to your first real incident report in 15 minutes.

---

## Part 1 — Getting Started (5 min)

### Step 1: Log in

Open your browser and go to the URL your administrator shared.

On your computer: use any browser.  
On your phone: bookmark it to your home screen (works like an app).

Your login credentials were sent to your email.

**Default credentials (change after first login):**  
Email: your admin email  
Password: the password you set during setup

---

### Step 2: Your first screen — the Command Center

When you log in as an admin or manager, you see the **Command Center**.

This shows:
- Total incidents and open cases across all your locations
- Any active escalations that need attention
- Safety signals — patterns detected by the system
- Automation events and workflow activity

**For field staff**, you'll land on the **My Shift** dashboard instead — a mobile-first view of your assignments.

---

### Step 3: Add your locations

Before reporting incidents, add your locations (called "centers").

1. Go to **Settings → Tenant Settings**
2. Or use the organization setup if you're building a multi-location hierarchy

Each location gets a short code (like `FL-MIA` or `NY-MAIN`). You'll use this when reporting incidents.

---

## Part 2 — Reporting an Incident (3 min)

This is the most important thing to get right. Make it easy for your team.

### On mobile

1. Open PackGuardian on your phone
2. Tap **Report Incident**
3. Choose the type: Dog Bite, Slip/Fall, Equipment, etc.
4. Answer the quick check-in questions (takes 30 seconds)
5. Use voice or type to describe what happened
6. Add photos if helpful
7. Tap **Submit Report**

That's it. A case is created automatically.

### What the AI does

When you submit a description, PackGuardian analyzes it to:
- Confirm the incident type
- Adjust severity if the description suggests something more serious
- Flag potential OSHA recordability
- Generate follow-up questions you may have missed

This is not mandatory — you can always override or ignore AI suggestions.

### Voice entry

The large microphone button is the fastest way to report.

Tap it and speak naturally:
> *"Kennel B-2, one of the dogs bit Marcus on the hand during feeding. Small puncture wound. We cleaned it and applied first aid. Marcus is back on duty."*

The system captures this and analyzes it automatically.

## Part 3 — Investigating a Case (5 min)

After a report is submitted, a **case** is automatically created.

### Finding cases

Click **Cases** in the navigation. You'll see all open cases for your organization.

Click any case to open the investigation workspace.

### The investigation workspace

At the top, you'll see the **Investigation Brief** — a summary of:
- What happened and where
- The operational risk score (0–100)
- What's driving the risk (contributing factors)
- Any operational patterns detected
- What the recommended next step is

### Tabs inside a case

| Tab | What it's for |
|-----|--------------|
| **Tasks** | Action items for the investigation team |
| **Corrective Actions** | Follow-up actions with due dates and owners |
| **Witnesses** | Structured witness statements |
| **Comments** | Internal notes with visibility controls |
| **Evidence** | Photos, documents, and AI-analyzed files |
| **Timeline** | Full event history of the case |
| **✦ Copilot** | AI assistant for investigation guidance |

### Corrective actions

When you identify what needs to change to prevent recurrence, add a **Corrective Action**:

1. Click the "Corrective Actions" tab
2. Click "+ Add Action"
3. Name the action, assign it to someone, set a due date
4. Choose a root cause category (training, equipment, process gap, etc.)

When the action is done, click the circle next to it to mark it complete.

## Part 4 — OSHA Compliance

### What PackGuardian tracks automatically

- **Recordability**: any incident with medical treatment beyond first aid, days away, or restricted work is flagged automatically
- **300 log**: recordable incidents appear in the OSHA 300 log automatically
- **301 forms**: individual incident detail forms are auto-populated from report data
- **300A annual summary**: generated at year end for posting

### What you still need to do manually

- Verify employee name, job title, and injury details are accurate
- Finalize the record once all information is confirmed
- Post the 300A summary between February 1 and April 30

## Part 5 — QR Codes (optional but powerful)

If you place QR codes at your locations, staff can scan them to:
- See recent incidents at that location
- Check open corrective actions
- Start a report pre-linked to that location

**To create a QR code:**
1. Go to Settings → QR Codes (or ask your admin)
2. Create one for each center, kennel, or equipment item
3. Print and laminate it — done

When a staff member scans it, they see an instant operational briefing for that location.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How do I know it saved?**  
When you submit a report, you see a green "Report Submitted" confirmation. If you're offline, you see "Saved offline — will sync when connected."

**What if I make a mistake on a report?**  
Open the case and edit the incident details from the investigation workspace. All changes are logged.

**Who gets notified when an incident is reported?**  
Managers assigned to the case and the safety director (if configured). Notification settings are in tenant settings.

**Can staff use it without creating an account?**  
Not currently — all reports require a logged-in user. This ensures proper attribution and audit trail.

**Does it work offline?**  
Yes. Reports submitted without a network connection are saved locally and sync automatically when connection is restored.

**What does the risk score mean?**  
The risk score (0–100) is for **operational prioritization**, not discipline. A score of 70+ means the incident warrants urgent follow-up. It's based on severity, OSHA exposure, escalation, repeat patterns, and unresolved actions.

**Can I export data?**  
OSHA forms can be viewed and printed from the OSHA section. PDF export of full case reports is on the roadmap.
